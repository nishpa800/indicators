This means THE_ONLY_ONE produces more signals and fewer breaks in an active Combo Chain run.

## csNew3_Bull sub-drift (secondary finding)

Inside the CC win-counting loop, squarify uses `csNew3_Bull` to decrement `cc_win_bull` when a UC combo fires (preventing double-count). The definition of `csNew3_Bull` itself differs:

| Location | csNew3_Bull definition |
|---|---|
| squarify (all 4 versions) | `csNew3_Bull = csNew1_Bull and nz(csNew2_Bull[1])` — lagged AND (previous bar's CS2) |
| hvd-pbj-ppd (all 4 versions) | `csNew3_Bull = csNew1_Bull and csNew2_Bull` — same-bar AND |

This is the same drift already cataloged in `docs/validation/2026-05-10-unified-combo-drift-1.md`. It propagates into the CC win-counter logic since squarify uses the lagged UC while hvd-pbj-ppd uses same-bar UC to track chain progression.
