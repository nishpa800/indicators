1. Mission — restated in one paragraph

The agentic OS is the externalization of Anish's trading cognition: a system where every important detection plot has a persistent owner agent, every important external data feed has a paired bull-case / bear-case interpreter, every fire of every plot is diagnosed by a grunt worker, debated by knowledge workers, collapsed to a four-square confidence matrix, stamped with an outcome, and forever-logged for calibration. It runs on the Claw Code stack (see strategy/2026-05-11-claw-code-foundation-plan.html) with multi-engine routing (cheap grunt engines + Opus knowledge engines + council mode for parity). It produces the highest-conviction trading signals for an alpha-max hyper-aggressive desk — and it does so as a repeatable system whose every step is named, scaffolded, calibrated, and version-controlled.

2. The five strategic pillars

The pillars are load-bearing structural elements, not bullets in a deck. Each pillar maps to a concrete skill that lives in .claude/skills/<name>/ and is invoked by a specific worker tier at a specific point in the system flow. Below each pillar I state the principle, its operational artifact, and its tier.

Figure 1 — The five pillars. Axis is grunt; Krishna + Shiva are knowledge; Premortem + Inversion + Pareto are owner-tier.

1. AXIS Principle. No fire, no setup, no strategy exists in one dimension. Decompose along the canonical axes before any judgment is made. Axes are independent — they should not co-vary in our analysis. Canonical axes (the 12 the OS uses today):
  • Timeframe — 1m / 5m / 15m / 1h / 1d
  • Direction — bull / bear
  • Structure — floor / 2nd floor / rooftop / penthouse / unconstrained
  • Momentum — displacement on / off; displacement direction
  • Volume tier — HV / HV1000 / HV150 / off
  • Regime — trend / range / gap-up / gap-down / inside-day
  • Session position — pre-market / open-30 / mid / power-hour / after-hours
  • Catalyst — earnings-window / news-window / none
  • Institutional positioning — top-quartile 13-F / bottom-quartile / no data
  • Options skew — heavy-call / heavy-put / balanced / unavailable
  • ETF rotation — sector flowing-in / flowing-out / neutral
  • GPS satellite agreement — 4/4 / 3/4 / 2/4 / 1/4 / 0/4 (per VOB-GPS-Four-Satellite)
Skill. .claude/skills/axis-decomposition/SKILL.md (grunt tier). Input: a fire event or a strategy proposal. Output: a structured axis-card with one value per axis.
Used by. Every Plot Owner before invoking the dialectic; every weekly strategy review by Owners. The axis-card is the structured prefix to every diagnosis card.
2. KRISHNA (steelman — preserver) Principle. Krishna is the eighth avatar of Vishnu, the Preserver. Strategist, statesman, philosopher (he delivered the Bhagavad Gita). Krishna sees through illusion to the long game. In our OS, Krishna is the meta-bull-case for a setup TYPE — not for a single fire (that's the per-fire bull-case agent), but for whether the strategy itself should exist, scale, or stay in the book. Skill. .claude/skills/krishna-steelman/SKILL.md (knowledge tier). Input: a strategy proposal or a setup type (e.g. "PBJ Bull on equities at open"). Output: the strongest, most generous, most dharmically-coherent case for adoption. Cite supporting structural facts, calibration history, and long-run analogues. Confidence: a number in [0, 1].
Used by. Owner-tier strategy reviews; new-plot canonicalization; quarterly book trim. Pairs with Shiva.
3. SHIVA (red team — destroyer) Principle. Shiva is the Destroyer (and the Transformer). The Tandava dance destroys to make way for new creation. Shiva confronts what must be unmade. In our OS, Shiva is the meta-bear-case for a setup TYPE — the systematic red-teaming of whether a strategy deserves to keep its slot in the book. Skill. .claude/skills/shiva-redteam/SKILL.md (knowledge tier). Input: same as Krishna. Output: the strongest case against the strategy. Cite contradicting calibration history, edge-case failure modes, regimes where the strategy systematically loses, and structural reasons it might be illusion-of-edge.
Used by. Same triggers as Krishna; the two ALWAYS run as a pair. The owner adjudicates by reading both outputs and applying premortem + inversion + Pareto downstream. Per SD-008, both sides use neutral framing in formal artifacts (no "good / evil") — the Krishna / Shiva nomenclature is reserved for skill names and conversation, not for formal labels in payloads.
4. PREMORTEM (failure-mode prep) Principle. Imagine the strategy / trade / setup HAS FAILED. Why? Enumerate the failure modes in plain text BEFORE we adopt. Pre-author the mitigation for each. The premortem is not "what might go wrong" — that's hand-waving. It's "this DID go wrong; what was the cause?" The discipline of writing in past-tense-of-failure surfaces causes that future-tense risk talk misses. Skill. .claude/skills/premortem/SKILL.md (owner tier). Input: a strategy proposal or a trade plan. Output: an ordered list of failure modes with severity, probability, mitigation, and an early-warning indicator for each.
Used by. Plot Owner before formally adopting a new detection plot into the live leaderboard. Daily before market open, the owner runs a "today-could-fail-because" premortem against the day's calendar (earnings, FOMC, jobs, CPI).
5. INVERSION (Munger: invert always invert) Principle. Munger's discipline: instead of asking "how do I succeed?", ask "what guarantees failure?" — then systematically avoid those things. The inversion produces a list of "never do" rules that are unconditional. They override every other consideration. Anish's existing inversions (codified): never modify .pine source (SD-002), never delete files (SD-004), never include phantom-toggle plots (SD-001 inversion), never tabulate concurrent fires on the alert bar (SD-011 inversion). Skill. .claude/skills/inversion/SKILL.md (owner tier). Input: a strategy or a domain. Output: a list of "never do" rules. Each rule has a clear scope, a violation detector, and an escalation path.
Used by. Owner-tier strategy reviews; appended to the STANDING_DECISIONS.md ledger when a new inversion is canonicalized.
6. PARETO LEVERAGE Principle. 80/20. Most alpha comes from a small subset of plots, fired in a small subset of conditions, against a small subset of symbols. The Pareto pillar scores every plot × symbol × TF × context cell on its contribution to realized alpha and ranks them. The system spends its compute, capital, and attention on the top 20% and treats the bottom 80% as automation candidates or retirement candidates. Skill. .claude/skills/pareto-leverage/SKILL.md (owner tier). Input: a slice of the calibration log (the per-fire payloads with outcome stamps, accumulated over time). Output: a Pareto ranking with leverage scores, and a Pareto frontier visual that names the 20% earning 80% of alpha.
Used by. Nightly across the portfolio. Weekly for each Plot Owner. Monthly for the strategy book overall. Promotion / demotion / retirement decisions for plots flow from the Pareto score.

5. GPS satellite system — promoted from deferred to scheduled

The four-satellite triangulation architecture is documented in docs/agentic-os/VOB_GPS_FOUR_SATELLITE.md. That doc previously had status: "deferred future work — flagged by Anish but not yet built." Figure 3 — GPS four-satellite triangulation. Each satellite reads the same bar through a different lens; agreement is informative because the lenses are decorrelated.

5.1 What the GPS layer adds — concretely

  • A new axis on the axis-card: satellite_agreement_count ∈ {0, 1, 2, 3, 4} and satellite_direction ∈ {bull, bear, mixed}.
  • A new grunt-tier skill: four-satellite-triangulator — given a bar, query Sat 1–4 and emit the agreement count + direction.
  • A new score in every per-fire payload: gps_conviction — derived from N-of-4 agreement, weighted by historical predictive value at that N.
  • A new long-run study (Stage 7 first-quarter): empirically tune the magic ratio — the (sat-3-sensitivity, sat-4-sensitivity) pair that maximizes decorrelation between Sat 3 and Sat 4 while keeping both reading the same market bar.

9. Strategy review chain — how the pillars compose at strategy time

This is the chain invoked for any strategy-level decision: new plot canonicalization, plot retirement, change in plot definition, addition of a new data source, modification of a standing decision. It does NOT run per-fire — it runs at strategy review cadence (weekly or on-demand).

Figure 5 — Strategy review chain. Axis → (Krishna || Shiva) → Premortem → Inversion → Pareto → Decision.

10. Per-fire chain — how the pillars compose at fire time

At fire time the chain is shorter (cost discipline; runs hundreds of times per day). The strategic principles are PRE-COMPUTED at strategy review time; per-fire we use their outputs (the never-do rules, the Pareto score) as guard-rails.

Figure 6 — Per-fire chain. Diagnosis → Context → Dialectic → Matrix → Outcome. Inversion rules gate the chain entrance.
