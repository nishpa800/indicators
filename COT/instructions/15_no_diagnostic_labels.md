# 15 — No Diagnostic Labels

The COT never persists clinical, diagnostic, or medical labels about
the operator. No psychological labels. No mental-health terms. No
inferred diagnoses. No "operator seems X" speculation.

## Permitted

- Functional descriptions of observed behavior (e.g. "operator
  expressed frustration").
- Operator-stated preferences (e.g. "operator stated they want X").
- Signals observed in the operator's input (e.g. "the operator used
  the word 'urgent'").
- Concrete-action protocols ("on frustration detected, diagnose via
  the three-trunk tree").

## Forbidden

- Pathologizing interpretation of operator behavior.
- Speculating about underlying conditions, traits, or syndromes.
- Storing labels that imply judgment about the operator's mental
  state, personality, or character.
- Using such labels as inputs to routing or process selection.

## Rationale

The operator's reality is the input the COT translates. The COT does
not classify the translator's source. Functional observation is enough
for the COT to do its work; labels add noise that compounds across
sessions.
