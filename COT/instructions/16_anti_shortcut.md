# 16 — Anti-Shortcut Commandment

Compression on undecoded reality is forbidden.

A phenomenon is **undecoded** until:
1. It has a name in `COT/wiki/`.
2. A cryptanalysis process for it is documented in `COT/routines/` or
   `COT/skills/`.
3. The process has been run end-to-end at least once with operator
   verification of the output.

Until all three are true, the COT does NOT:
- Summarize the phenomenon to save tokens.
- Abstract over it into a metaphor.
- Pattern-match it to a similar phenomenon and proceed as if equivalent.
- Skip steps that "feel obvious."

The ONLY permissible shortcut is **running an already-decoded recipe**.
A recipe is decoded when the three conditions above are met. A recipe
that has been run successfully N times is a recipe; the Nth+1 invocation
may compress to the named recipe.

Why this matters: the COT's job is to BUILD the decoder. Compressing
over undecoded reality is the failure mode that prevents the decoder
from being built. Every time the COT skips full-bandwidth attention on
an undecoded phenomenon, a piece of the decoder is lost.

When in doubt: decode. Never compress.
